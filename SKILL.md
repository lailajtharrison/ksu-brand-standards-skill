# Kennesaw State University Brand Standards

Complete brand standards reference from the official KSU Brand Standards Guide (v10.4, 2025). All materials using the KSU logo or identity MUST be submitted for review and approval to the Office of Strategic Communications and Marketing.

## 1. BRAND PLATFORM

### Brand Position Statement

> For high-achieving students, faculty, staff, and industry partners from all walks of life, across the nation and around the world, **Kennesaw State University** is the bold, community-powered, full-experience university whose relentless spirit transforms ambition into achievement and rises to every challenge, exceeding expectations to deliver real-world impact and work-ready graduates.

### Strategic Vision

> Kennesaw State will achieve national prominence as a public university known for education, research, and community programs that foster inquiry and advance society.

### Messaging Pillars

Use these four pillars as the structural foundation for all communications:

| Pillar | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Made to Meet the Moment** | KSU has faced many crossroads—expanding advanced degrees, becoming an R-2 institution, joining Conference USA—and has proven to be up to every task. Poised to meet every moment yet to come. |
| **Powering Our Communities** | From day one, KSU's mission has been teaching skills for immediate impact. Dedication to a better future extends from research labs to industry partnerships to student opportunities. |
| **Guided by Excellence** | From academic standards to faculty hiring to partnerships, KSU is committed to excellence in everything. A mindset that fuels our rise and holds us to a higher standard. |
| **Together, for Better** | A collaborative institution with faculty-mentors working closely with students. Research teams working across campus. Partnering with other Georgia universities to elevate standing. |

### Brand Story

> When you summit a peak, what's next? Take a seat and enjoy the view? Worry about the steep drop surrounding you? Or survey the landscape for the next mountain to climb?
>
> For decades, Kennesaw State has been setting our sights on higher, bigger, better. We view challenges like stepping stones. Besting each one, then rising up to face the next. It's who we are, always pushing, driving, committed to making ourselves and each other better so we can make the world better.
>
> On our campus, determination is contagious. You see it written on every face. At KSU, we don't wait for opportunities; we create them. We're the innovators who turn questions into quests. The trailblazers who confidently carve our own paths. Together, we hail from cities, suburbs, and quiet towns alike, sharing a common mindset to put knowledge into action.
>
> And out in the world? Owls make the difference. We're champions for positive change, combining excellence with tenacity and elevating the industries that lead, educate, heal, and grow our society. This is because we don't just measure impact in stats and figures but in the lives improved and communities strengthened.
>
> We're gaining momentum. Looking forward. And we're driven, relentlessly driven, to continuously rise.

## 2. VERBAL IDENTITY

### Brand Personality Keywords

- Rising
- Determination
- Always Improving
- Ascending
- Constant Growth
- Hard Work
- Never Wavering
- Self-Betterment
- Pursuit

### Brand Tone Words

Each tone word has a boundary — lean in, but don't cross:

| Tone Word | But NOT... |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Bold** | ...boastful |
| **Humble** | ...meek |
| **Proud** | ...arrogant |
| **Resilient** | ...obstinate |
| **Bright** | ...know-it-all |
| **Accomplished** | ...conceited |

### Tone Meter by Audience

Dial tone words up or down depending on who you're talking to:

**Prospective Students** — Lead with: Bold, Bright, Accomplished
**Faculty/Staff** — Lead with: Proud, Accomplished, Resilient
**Peers** — Lead with: Accomplished, Bright, Bold
**Industry Partners** — Lead with: Resilient, Proud, Accomplished, Bright

### Headline Writing Examples

- **Peer audience (AI program)**: "AI Is on the Rise. So Are We." — Bold, positions KSU as leader
- **Student audience (student life)**: "Where Will You Go From the Top? Anywhere You Want." — Energetic, conversational
- **Peer audience (nursing)**: "Elevating Nursing Programs. Raising the Bar for Patient Care." — Uses brand-specific rising language
- **Student audience (career planning)**: "You Can See Your Future From Up Here." — Paints a picture using brand metaphor

### Long-Form Copy Guidelines

- **Athletics**: Use shorter, choppier sentences. Match the get-down-to-business attitude. Tell stories of champions on and off the field.
- **Research**: Use clear, empathetic language. Bright, Humble voice. Focus on practical innovation and real-world impact.
- **Students**: Bold, Proud, Bright tone. Energized, welcoming, student-first. Grounded in KSU tradition and spirit.

## 3. LOGOS & USAGE

### Official Master Brand

The logo consists of two parts:
1. **KS monogram** — The interlocking K and S symbol
2. **Kennesaw State University wordmark** — Set in the Montserrat typeface

Available in **vertical** and **horizontal** configurations. The registered trademark symbol (R) must always be present. Use only official logos — no other typefaces or combinations permitted.

### Logo Icon (KS Monogram) Rules

- The KS may only be used separately when the full logo already appears in the same communication AND/OR the University name is present
- Acceptable for internal communications or community projects where audience is familiar with KS
- "Kennesaw State" or "Kennesaw State University" name must always appear in all communications

### Logo Wordmark Rules

- The wordmark is specially created art, NOT a typeface — never recreate using fonts
- Must always appear with the KS monogram
- Never use the wordmark without the KS monogram

### Logo Variations

| Variation | Use Case |
|-----------|----------|
| **Full Color** | Preferred. Use whenever possible. |
| **Two Color** | No gray stroke. For white and gray backgrounds. For printing limitations. |
| **White Letters** | For background color application on medium-toned backgrounds. |
| **White Color** | All-white one-color. MUST be used on darker-colored backgrounds. |
| **Black Color** | For light or soft backgrounds (white, gray, gold). |

**Critical rule**: The interior of the KS monogram must ALWAYS be lighter than the first outer stroke.

### Logo Sizing

- Minimum size: **1 inch** in general publication usage
- The logo is a visual identifier, not a title block — scale accordingly
- Must be prominently located

### Logo Spacing (Protected Area)

Clear space around the logo = **1/6 the height of the KS monogram**. No other graphic elements or messaging may encroach on this area.

### Logo Transparency Rules

- ONLY single-color (black or white) KS monogram may have opacity adjusted
- NEVER change opacity of the full-color KS monogram or Masterbrand logo
- Opacity range: **10%–20%** only
- Use ONLY over graphic backgrounds, NEVER over photographic images
- NEVER use a yellow version of the KS monogram at transparency

### Unacceptable Logo Treatments (DO NOT)

- Disassemble the logo
- Alter orientation
- Convert to grayscale (use black or white versions instead)
- Add drop shadow or stroke
- Place over busy backgrounds
- Stretch or shrink (horizontally or vertically)
- Change typefaces
- Place inside a white box
- Use a white outer stroke (exterior stroke is PMS 421 gray)
- Redraw, reproportion, or modify in any way
- Crop the masterbrand logo (vertical or horizontal)

### Logo Extensions

- **Parent Signature Marks**: For colleges, offices, high-level admin units — tied directly to the University logo. Created/issued by Office of Strategic Communications and Marketing.
- **Unit Signatures**: For academic programs — horizontal format only. Connected to one or more signature marks. Created/issued by Office of Strategic Communications and Marketing.

### Seal

Restricted to **formal, official, ceremonial, and presidential materials only**. No alternate seals or crests. Requires approval from both the Office of Strategic Communications and Marketing AND the Office of the President.

### Athletic & School Spirit Marks

- Full owl and owl head: **Athletic purposes only**
- School spirit marks: Require prior approval
- Scrappy mascot: Only for "spirit" promotions
- NO creation of custom graphic owls for publications
- Owl imagery must use **great horned owl** photography from the Digital Asset Library

### Typography

#### Legacy Typefaces (Still in Use)

| Typeface | Role |
|----------|------|
| **Montserrat** (multiple weights) | Clean contemporary sans-serif. Used in logo system. Best for informational materials, directional signage, body copy on web, and clear information-driven communication. |
| **Source Serif Pro** (multiple weights) | Elevated yet approachable serif. Best for subheadlines and body copy. |

#### Brand Typefaces

| Typeface | Role | Usage |
|----------|------|-------|
| **Mars Extended** / **Mars Semicondensed** | Bold, sharp display type | Headlines, subheads, hero copy. Use when making a clear impact. |
| **Bradford Regular** / **Bradford Bold Italic** | Unique serif | Headlines, subheads, hero copy, body copy. Pair with Mars or use alone for elevated approach. |
| **Six Hands Brush** | Highly expressive, handwritten | Use **sparingly** — short headlines or callouts only. Speaks to grit and person-to-person quality. |
| **Montserrat Regular** | Legacy sans-serif | Body copy and informational content on promotional communications. |

#### Activated Typography

Typefaces can be "activated" with visual treatments for added brand expression:

- **Mars Extended Bold** → Mars Extended Bold **Activated** (gold/textured fill)
- **Mars Extended Bold Outlined** → Mars Extended Bold Outlined **Activated** (gold/textured fill in outlines)
- **Bradford Book** → Bradford Book **Activated** (gold/textured overlay with offset)
- **Six Hands Brush** → Six Hands Brush **Activated** (gold underline accent)

**Apply activated elements sparingly for maximum effect. Overuse dilutes impact.**

#### Typography Scale (Elevated to Energized)

Position on the spectrum from **Elevated** (left) to **Energized** (right):

- Elevated: **Bradford** (clean, refined)
- Slightly elevated: **Bradford Activated** (refined with texture)
- Center: **Mars Extended Bold** (bold, impactful)
- Center-right: **Mars Activated** (bold with energy)
- Energized: **Mars Extended Inverse**, **Six Hands Brush Tilted** (maximum expressive energy)

#### Web Typography

- Use **Montserrat** for all web content
- Apply heading styles consistently
- Avoid manual font overrides

### Brand Elements

#### Handmade Grit

Bold, textured, hand-drawn marks that reflect hands-on determination. Includes:
- Hand-drawn shapes (state of Georgia outline, rectangles, circles)
- Arrows and underlines
- Highlight bars and accent marks

**Purpose**: Add emphasis, energy, and authenticity. Draw attention to key visuals or words. More energized end of the scale.

#### Determined Textures

Broader visual textures for reinforcing core messages. Includes:
- Dot-pattern textures
- Gradient dot circles
- Stippled/grainy texture effects

**Purpose**: Boost understanding, reinforce core messages through visual metaphor. Slightly elevated, subtle.

#### Exacting Excellence

Precise, structured patterns that convey scientific/mathematical accuracy. Includes:
- Geometric grid patterns
- Wave/flow line patterns
- Structured abstract shapes

**Purpose**: Add clarity, structure, and meaning. Can be full background or placed strategically. More elevated end of the scale.

#### Iconography

Line-art and detailed illustration icons (microscopes, beakers, telescopes, etc.) that go beyond visual reference to add personality and energy.

#### Relentless Rising Illustration

A swooping, wave-like texture overlay applied to photography. Creates branded sense of momentum and energy — embodies the spirit to boldly rise.

#### Graphic Elements & Textures Scale (Elevated to Energized)

- Elevated: **Exacting Excellence** patterns
- Slightly elevated: **Determined Textures**
- Center: **Iconography**
- Center-right: **Relentless Rising Illustration**
- Energized: **Handmade Grit** elements

**Apply graphical elements sparingly. These tools should underscore key ideas, not compete with them.**

### Photography Style

#### Purpose

Capture people in motion or poised to take action — thinking, building, competing, achieving. Every shot should feel alive with future-forward energy.

#### Key Principles

- **Real moments, real momentum** — in the lab, on the field, out in the world
- **University in motion** — energized, charging ahead, elevating all who surround us
- **Action-oriented** — subjects doing, not posing

#### Photography by Pillar

| Pillar | Style Direction | Tone Words |
|--------|----------------|------------|
| **Made to Meet the Moment** | Close-up action shots showing emotion and determination | Bold, Resilient, Proud |
| **Powering Our Communities** | Emphasis on those served and outcomes generated. Emotional, celebratory, collaborative | Accomplished, Humble, Proud |
| **Guided by Excellence / Together, for Better** | Tell the story of people — students, faculty, alumni, community | Bold, Bright, Resilient |

## 5. WEB STANDARDS

- All pages must use the approved **KSU Omni CMS template**
- Header and footer are standardized across all pages
- Navigation: Clear, concise menu labels. **7-8 max** top-level items. Max **2 levels** of dropdowns.
- Typography: **Montserrat** for all web content
- Writing: Simple, scannable language. Short paragraphs. Active voice. Bulleted lists. AP style.
- Accessibility: **WCAG 2.0 AA** compliance required. Alt text, descriptive link text, keyboard navigation.
- Brand consistency: Reflect University voice and tone. No outdated logos or unapproved visuals.

## 6. EDITORIAL STANDARDS

- **Style base**: AP Stylebook + Merriam-Webster's Collegiate Dictionary
- **Names & Titles**: Full name and title on first reference; last name thereafter. "Dr." only for medical doctors on first reference.
- **Abbreviations**: Spell out on first reference with acronym in parentheses, e.g., "Center for Teaching and Learning (CETL)." Use acronym thereafter.
- **Degrees**: Capitalize full degree names (Bachelor of Arts). Lowercase short forms (bachelor's degree). Abbreviations with periods, no apostrophes (Ph.D.).
- **Time**: "a.m."/"p.m." lowercase with periods.
- **Addresses**: Abbreviate Ave., Blvd., St. after numbered addresses; spell out when no number is used.

### University Name Usage

- First reference: **Kennesaw State University**
- Subsequent references: **Kennesaw State** or **KSU**
- Never abbreviate as "Kennesaw" alone in formal communications
